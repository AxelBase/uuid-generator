UUID v4 vs v7: Should You Switch?

Published: November 22, 2025

UUID v7 (introduced 2024) adds time-ordering while keeping uniqueness. Sounds perfect — but is it?

Quick Comparison

v4 (Random)v7 (Time-ordered)
UniquenessNearly infiniteHigh (with counter)
PrivacyPerfectLeaky (timestamp)
Index performancePoor (fragmented)Excellent (sequential)
Offline safeYesRisk of collision
AdoptionUniversalEmerging

Why v4 Still Wins for Most Teams

  • Zero information leakage
  • Works perfectly offline or distributed
  • Supported everywhere since 2005
  • Simpler implementation

Use v7 Only If You Need

B-tree index performance at scale — and you accept timestamp exposure.

For 99% of applications — including this generator — v4 remains the correct, future-proof choice.
